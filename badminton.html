Badminton

Rules

  • Scoring: Badminton matches are usually played to the best of three games. The first player or team to reach 21 points wins a game. If the score reaches 20-20, the game continues until one side has a two-point lead.
  • Service: The server must stand within their service court (diagonally opposite to the receiver's court) and hit the shuttlecock diagonally over the net into the opponent's service court.
  • Rally: After the service, players hit the shuttlecock back and forth over the net until one side fails to return it or the shuttlecock lands out of bounds. The shuttlecock can only be hit once before it passes over the net.
  • Scoring: A player or team scores a point when the opponent commits a fault (e.g., the shuttlecock lands out of bounds, fails to clear the net, or is struck before it crosses the net).
  • Faults: Common faults in badminton include:
    1. Failure to hit the shuttlecock over the net into the opponent's court
    2. Hitting the shuttlecock out of bounds.
